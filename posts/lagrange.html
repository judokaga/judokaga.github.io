Lagrange's Theorem

Posted on September 17, 2017 by Judokaga

Theorem (Lagrange):

If \(H\) is a subgroup of group \(G\), then \([G:H] = o(G) / o(H)\).

Proof:

We prove there is a bijection between every left coset in \(G / H\) and \(H\). The relationship of \(m \in G\) and \(n \in H\) is \(x^{-1}m = h\), when the left coset is \(x / H\). If \(m_1, m_2 \in x/H, m_1 \neq m_2\), then \(x^{-1}m_1 \neq x^{-1}m_2\). If \(h_1, h_2 \in H, h_1 \neq h_2\), then \(xh_1 \neq xh_2\).

So every left coset in \(G / H\) have the same number of elements, so \([G:H] \times o(H) = o(G)\).
